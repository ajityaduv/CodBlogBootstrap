Begining with Python:The easiest and most powerful Programming Language

Python is really easy to pick up and learn, that is why a lot of people recommend Python to beginners. You need less lines of code to perform the same task as compared to other major languages like C/C++ and Java. Python is a very productive language. Due to the simplicity of Python, developers can focus on solving the problem. It comes under the OSI approved open-source license. This makes it free to use and distribute.

Learning a New Skill: Trying my Hands on Adobe Photoshop

Photoshop was one of the firts softwares that I learned and actively used it in my daily tasks and Projects. With Photoshop you aren’t limited by funds, tools, or experience. The program is relatively easy to use and, like anything else, the more you practice, the more you can create. The list of Photoshop features is virtually endless, and each new feature you try out gives you a new opportunity to improve your skills and flex your creative muscles.

And lastly, MS Excel: The most powerful and widely used Microsoft Software

It was peer to peer learning. I learned it from one of my friends and made some advanced Spreadsheets. Excel is a powerful tool for building bespoke template-based spreadsheets for use in business, for analysis of data and for presenting data graphically. We have helped many clients use it in their business to great effect, but here are the benefits of Excel according to Microsoft Working with massive amounts of data, which supports spreadsheets that can be up to 1 million rows by 16,000 columns. In addition to the bigger grid, Excel supports multicore processor platforms for faster calculation of formula-intense spreadsheets.
